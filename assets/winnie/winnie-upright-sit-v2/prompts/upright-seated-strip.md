# Winnie upright seated loop

Use case: stylized-concept
Asset type: six-frame desktop-pet animation strip

Create one coherent horizontal strip of exactly six separated full-body poses of Winnie, all on a perfectly flat solid `#FF00FF` chroma-key background.

## Reference roles

- `winnie-tnr-canonical-base.png`: authoritative identity, rendering style, markings, colors, collar, tag, eyes, proportions, tail, and TNR ear tip.
- `winnie-front-current.png` and `winnie-three-quarter-current.png`: current Winnie body proportions and appearance from front and three-quarter views.
- `ajman-upright-sit-silhouette.png`: posture reference only. Borrow the compact straight-on upright seated silhouette, paired front paws, and vertical composure. Do not borrow Ajman's colors, markings, face, or proportions.
- `winnie-loaf-existing.png`: exclusion/context reference only. The new pose must be a true upright sit, visibly distinct from this existing loaf.
- `six-frame-layout.png`: layout-only reference for six equal slots, consistent scale, baseline, centering, and safe padding. Do not reproduce its boxes, guides, lines, gray background, or labels.

## Identity lock

Winnie is a realistic compact brown mackerel tabby with warm tan-brown fur, dark black-brown stripes, a clear forehead M and cheek stripes, yellow-green eyes, a pink collar, one round silver tag, and a dark ringed tail. Preserve her actual face and slightly sturdy domestic-cat proportions. Her own left ear has a real flat TNR rescue tip; when she faces forward this is the ear on the viewer's right. The opposite ear remains naturally pointed. The TNR asymmetry must be visible in all six frames.

## Pose and motion

Winnie sits fully upright and faces nearly straight toward the viewer, like the Ajman posture reference. Her hindquarters rest on the ground, torso vertical, both front legs straight, and front paws paired neatly at the same baseline. She must not stand, walk, crouch, loaf, or angle her whole body sideways. Keep her tail attached and wrapped naturally around or just beside the front paws.

Build a quiet seamless six-frame loop:

1. neutral upright sit, alert eyes
2. tiny inhale: chest rises slightly, ears attentive
3. slow blink beginning
4. eyes softly closed, minimal breathing lift
5. eyes open, very small head/ear adjustment and tail-tip shift
6. settle almost exactly back toward frame 1 for a smooth loop

Motion is subtle and anatomical. Keep the head size, body volume, paw placement, baseline, collar, tag, markings, lighting, camera, and scale consistent. All six frames must look like the same Winnie in the same seated pose, with visible but restrained breathing, blink, ear, and tail-tip variation.

## Production constraints

- exactly six complete, separated poses, left to right
- one consistent full-body scale and ground baseline
- generous padding; no pose touches the canvas edge or overlaps another slot
- flat uniform `#FF00FF` only behind the cat
- no shadows, floor plane, scenery, text, labels, borders, guides, numbers, or checkerboard
- no motion marks, sparkles, effects, props, duplicate tags, or detached fur/whisker fragments
- no white, gray, black, gradient, textured, or transparent background
- no cropped ears, paws, tail, collar, or tag
- no identity drift, generic tabby substitution, missing TNR tip, swapped ear tip, changed collar, or changed eye color

The result should read at small desktop-pet size as Winnie calmly sitting upright, not as a portrait sheet or six unrelated cat variations.
