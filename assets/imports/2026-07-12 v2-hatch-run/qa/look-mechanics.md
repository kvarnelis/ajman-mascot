# Ajman look mechanics

Ajman stays seated and grounded, with both white front paws and the lower torso fixed to one stable baseline. His yellow-green eyes lead each gaze, followed by a restrained turn or pitch of the head and a slight ear follow-through; the torso remains nearly still. Preserve the same eye construction, tuxedo markings, white muzzle and chest, black ears, face proportions, and calm expression. The eyes rotate naturally within their existing apertures while eyelids subtly reshape; do not add replacement eyes or slide detached pupils over a fixed face.

The motion budget is even: each 22.5-degree step changes eye aim and head orientation by roughly the same small amount. The face must remain legible and Ajman's body scale and paws must not shift.

- 000 up: frontal body; chin lifts slightly; pupils and eye highlights rise; upper face and both ears remain visible.
- 090 screen-right: head yaws toward screen-right; nose and pupils cross right of head center; the screen-left cheek becomes more visible and the far cheek is slightly occluded.
- 180 down: frontal body; chin lowers toward the white chest; pupils lower and upper eyelids follow; ears stay attached and symmetric enough to preserve identity.
- 270 screen-left: inverse of 090; head yaws toward screen-left; nose and pupils cross left of head center; the screen-right cheek becomes more visible and the far cheek is slightly occluded.

Ajman has no separate prop. Whiskers remain attached and follow the muzzle continuously without flipping or changing count conspicuously.
